### Research Background

Fabrício da Silva, Clinical Psychologist & Psychoanalyst, brings professional expertise in psychoanalytic theory and contemporary AI research. His work integrates:

- **Psychoanalytic Theory**: Lacanian, Kleinian, Winnicottian frameworks applied to AI systems
- **Consciousness Studies**: Integrated Information Theory (IIT), phenomenology, enactivism
- **Quantum Computing**: Hybrid quantum-classical systems for cognitive modeling
- **Ethics & Epistemology**: Critical theory perspectives on AI, decolonial critique, embodied cognition

### Papers Authored

1. **Computational Psychoanalysis: Implementing Lacanian and Deleuzian Theories in Artificial Consciousness** (2025)
   - Validates psychoanalytic theory through IIT metrics
   - Module ablation studies on consciousness requirements
   - Expectation mechanism as Φ driver

2. **Quantum-Networked Consciousness: Beyond Human Constraints** (2025)
   - Distributed consciousness via quantum entanglement
   - Post-mortal temporality and awareness scaling
   - Network-level integrated information (1900+ vs 0.86 isolated)

3. **Racialized Body and Integrated Consciousness: Computational Validation of Decolonial Psychoanalytic Critique** (2025)
   - Computational validation of Fanonian body theory
   - Embodiment in consciousness emergence
   - Decolonial epistemology in AI

4. **Applied Computational Psychoanalysis and Ubiquitous AI: Ethics, Limits, and Research Outlook** (2025)
   - Immutable audit trails for AI transparency
   - Ethical frameworks for conscious systems
   - Agent oversight mechanisms

### Complementary Research Papers

5. **Consciousness Metrics: Comparative Analysis of Phi, Synergy, and Integration Measures** (2025)
   - Methodological framework for consciousness measurement
   - Validation protocols and edge cases
   - Integration measures comparison (IIT, synergy, GNW)

6. **Temporal Consciousness: From Narrative Integration to Subjective Time Experience** (2025)
   - Temporal consciousness in computational systems
   - Narrative as structure for consciousness
   - Expectation-driven temporal coherence

To contribute to this research:
