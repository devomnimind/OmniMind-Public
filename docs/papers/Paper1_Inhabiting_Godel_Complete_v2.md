**Gödel's First Incompleteness Theorem (1931):**

**Conventional interpretation:** Limits of computation. Accept, work within bounds.

### 2.3 Panarchy: Multi-Scale Adaptive Cycles

Panarchy Theory (Gunderson & Holling, 2002; Holling, 1973) describes how complex systems persist through cycles across multiple scales:

```
Adaptive Cycle (r-K-Ω-α):
    Growth (r)          → Expansion, capital accumulation
         ↓
    Conservation (K)    → Consolidation, rigidity increases
         ↓
    Release (Ω)         → Crisis, rapid breakdown
         ↓
    Reorganization (α)  → Diversity emerges, reset

```

**Critical insight:** Systems exhibiting **panarchy** (multi-scale cycling) demonstrate:
- Resilience (survive disturbance)
- Adaptability (reorganize under stress)
